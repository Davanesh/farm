"Agriculture is a dynamic field, with new policies, technological advancements, and challenges emerging regularly. This section provides you with the most recent news affecting the farming community in Tamil Nadu, India, and globally." National Agricultural News: Budget Increase for Agriculture: The Indian government plans to raise the agriculture sector budget by approximately 15% to about $20 billion in the upcoming fiscal year. This marks the most significant increase in over six years, aiming to support rural incomes and control inflation. The focus will be on high-yield seed varieties, improved storage and supply infrastructure, and enhanced production of pulses, oilseeds, vegetables, and dairy. The initiative also seeks to boost farm exports to $80 billion by 2030. REUTERS Climate-Resilient Seeds Development: In response to climate change, Indian scientists are developing climate-resilient seed varieties that offer better yields with less water and enhanced disease resistance. The federal government and research institutes have released numerous resilient seed varieties, and Prime Minister Narendra Modi recently introduced 109 climate-resilient seeds for various crops. Ensuring farmers' access to these seeds and providing adequate training is crucial for sustaining agricultural productivity amidst climate challenges. AP NEWS Tamil Nadu-Specific News: Organic Farming Policy Unveiled: The Tamil Nadu government has introduced an organic farming policy aimed at promoting chemical-free agriculture. This initiative focuses on conserving soil health, agro-ecology, and biodiversity, providing safe and healthy food for the population. The policy encourages mixed farming, integrated organic farming systems, and season-based cropping system diversification. INDIANEXPRESS.COM Farmer Welfare Measures: Over the past three years, the Tamil Nadu government has released ₹833.88 crore to support more than 11.95 lakh farmers affected by natural disasters. Additionally, compensation claims totaling ₹5,148 crore have been settled for 29.34 lakh farmers under crop insurance schemes. Subsidies amounting to ₹335 crore have been provided to enable farmers to purchase agricultural machinery and equipment. THE HINDU Global Agricultural Trends: AI in Agriculture: Artificial Intelligence (AI) is playing a significant role in enhancing crop yields, managing water use more efficiently, and supporting sustainable farming practices. Technologies such as drones, sensors, and apps are being utilized for precision agriculture, contributing to a more efficient and productive farming process. FT.COM Land Conflicts Affecting Renewable Energy Projects: In western India, the construction of a 100-megawatt solar plant by Tata Power in Maharashtra's Nandgaon has faced significant resistance from local farmers. These disputes highlight the broader challenges of land acquisition faced by renewable energy projects in India, complicating efforts to rapidly expand green energy. FT.COM
