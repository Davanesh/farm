Latest Agricultural News

Stay Updated on Agricultural Trends and Updates


"Agriculture is a dynamic field, with new policies, technological advancements, and challenges emerging regularly. This section provides you with the most recent news affecting the farming community in Tamil Nadu, India, and globally."

National Agricultural News:
Budget Increase for Agriculture: The Indian government plans to raise the agriculture sector budget by approximately 15% to about $20 billion in the upcoming fiscal year. This marks the most significant increase in over six years, aiming to support rural incomes and control inflation. The focus will be on high-yield seed varieties, improved storage and supply infrastructure, and enhanced production of pulses, oilseeds, vegetables, and dairy. The initiative also seeks to boost farm exports to $80 billion by 2030.
Climate-Resilient Seeds Development: In response to climate change, Indian scientists are developing climate-resilient seed varieties that offer better yields with less water and enhanced disease resistance. The federal government and research institutes have released numerous resilient seed varieties, and Prime Minister Narendra Modi recently introduced 109 climate-resilient seeds for various crops. Ensuring farmers' access to these seeds and providing adequate training is crucial for sustaining agricultural productivity amidst climate challenges.

Tamil Nadu-Specific News:
Organic Farming Policy Unveiled: The Tamil Nadu government has introduced an organic farming policy aimed at promoting chemical-free agriculture. This initiative focuses on conserving soil health, agro-ecology, and biodiversity, providing safe and healthy food for the population. The policy encourages mixed farming, integrated organic farming systems, and season-based cropping system diversification.
Farmer Welfare Measures: Over the past three years, the Tamil Nadu government has released ₹833.88 crore to support more than 11.95 lakh farmers affected by natural disasters. Additionally, compensation claims totaling ₹5,148 crore have been settled for 29.34 lakh farmers under crop insurance schemes. Subsidies amounting to ₹335 crore have been provided to enable farmers to purchase agricultural machinery and equipment.

Global Agricultural Trends:
AI in Agriculture: Artificial Intelligence (AI) is playing a significant role in enhancing crop yields, managing water use more efficiently, and supporting sustainable farming practices. Technologies such as drones, sensors, and apps are being utilized for precision agriculture, contributing to a more efficient and productive farming process.
Land Conflicts Affecting Renewable Energy Projects: In western India, the construction of a 100-megawatt solar plant by Tata Power in Maharashtra's Nandgaon has faced significant resistance from local farmers. These disputes highlight the broader challenges of land acquisition faced by renewable energy projects in India, complicating efforts to rapidly expand green energy.

Success Stories

Learn from Farmers Who Made a Difference


1. The Organic Revolution by Selvaraj, Tamil Nadu Selvaraj, a farmer from Madurai, turned his failing farm into a thriving organic venture. He began using natural compost and pest repellents like neem oil. Within three years, his soil quality improved, and his yields doubled. His organic produce is now sold at premium rates, earning him twice the income compared to chemical-based farming. Lesson: Embrace organic farming for long-term benefits and healthier produce.

2. Dr. Murugan’s Millets Mission Dr. Murugan from TNAU advocated for millet cultivation in drought-prone areas. A group of farmers followed his advice and switched to millet farming. The crops required less water and grew well even during erratic rains. The farmers not only secured their livelihoods but also introduced nutritious food to local markets. Lesson: Millets are a sustainable crop that ensures income even in tough climatic conditions.

3. Vandana Shiva’s Seed Saving Success Vandana Shiva promoted seed banks among rural women in Tamil Nadu. Women started saving traditional seed varieties, which were drought-resistant and required fewer inputs. The initiative not only preserved biodiversity but also reduced farmers' dependency on costly hybrid seeds. Lesson: Saving native seeds can build resilience against climate change.

4. A Young Farmer’s Tech Success Ajay, a 25-year-old farmer from Coimbatore, used AI-based apps to monitor soil health and weather patterns. By implementing precision farming techniques, he reduced fertilizer use by 30% and increased his crop yield by 40%. His innovative methods earned him recognition and a government subsidy for smart farming tools. Lesson: Technology in agriculture is a game-changer.

5. Rani’s Kitchen Garden Transformation Rani, a homemaker-turned-farmer from Tirunelveli, started a kitchen garden using organic waste from her home. She grew vegetables like tomatoes, spinach, and brinjal. Her small initiative inspired her community, leading to the creation of a collective organic vegetable market. Lesson: Small steps like kitchen gardening can create a big impact.

6. Selvam’s Fight Against Cyclone Damage After Cyclone Gaja, Selvam from Nagapattinam faced devastating crop losses. With guidance from local agricultural officers, he switched to short-duration crops like pulses. He recovered his losses within one season and now practices multi-cropping to minimize risks. Lesson: Adaptation and resilience are key to overcoming farming challenges.

7. Suganya’s Drip Irrigation Triumph Suganya from Theni faced water scarcity on her farm. She installed a drip irrigation system with government support, reducing water usage by 60%. This allowed her to grow high-value crops like capsicum and strawberries, earning her a steady income. Lesson: Smart water management can transform farming outcomes.
