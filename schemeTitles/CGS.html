Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana (RKVY): Aims to increase the productivity of Indian agriculture by funding innovations in farming and providing financial support for state-level initiatives. Key Benefits: Assistance for crop diversification, organic farming, and setting up agro-industries. Unique Feature: Focuses on funding state-specific agricultural needs. National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture (NMSA): Focuses on making farming climate-resilient through water-efficient irrigation, soil health management, and agroforestry. Key Benefits: Subsidies for micro-irrigation, soil testing, and planting trees on farmland. Unique Feature: Supports integrated farming systems combining crops, livestock, and fishery. Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY): Promotes organic farming practices and certification. It empowers farmers to form clusters and grow chemical-free produce. Key Benefits: Financial assistance for organic farming and market linkage for organic products. Unique Feature: Cluster-based approach for community-level implementation. Kisan Urja Suraksha evam Utthaan Mahabhiyan (KUSUM): Encourages farmers to adopt solar power by providing subsidies for solar panels and pumps. Farmers can also sell surplus energy to the grid. Key Benefits: Reduces irrigation costs and promotes renewable energy adoption. Unique Feature: Farmers earn additional income through energy production.
